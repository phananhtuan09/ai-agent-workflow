# Review Rubric — Landing Page Showcase

Use this rubric to evaluate a high-end showcase landing page that is meant to demonstrate visual craft, motion, interaction quality, and technical polish.

Pair this file with:

- `scoring-policy.md` for status rules and total calculation
- `scoring-anchors.md` for anchor calibration
- `evidence-matrix.md` for evidence requirements
- `anti-bias-rules.md` to avoid halo effects
- `output-template.md` for final report structure

---

## Scoring Method

Rate each category from `0` to `5`:

- `0` = Missing
- `1` = Very weak
- `2` = Basic
- `3` = Good
- `4` = Strong
- `5` = Excellent

Weighted score formula:

```text
category_score = (rating / 5) * weight
```

Maximum total: `100`

Do not decide score eligibility from this file alone. Apply:

- status rules from `scoring-policy.md`
- confidence ceilings from `evidence-matrix.md`
- top-tier eligibility rules from `scoring-policy.md`

---

## 1. Creative Direction — Weight: 7

Check:

- The page has a clear creative concept.
- The intended mood is obvious.
- The visual direction feels intentional, not random.
- There is a strong art direction behind it.
- The page follows one design language.

## 2. Brand / Identity Expression — Weight: 7

Check:

- The page has a distinct personality.
- The identity is memorable.
- The design feels unique rather than trend-copying.
- The page communicates who the brand or studio is.
- The style stays consistent from top to bottom.

## 3. Hero Section Impact — Weight: 8

Check:

- The first screen creates immediate impact.
- The headline is strong and well presented.
- The hero has a clear focal point.
- Hero visuals and motion feel polished.
- The hero gives a reason to keep scrolling.

## 4. Storytelling / Section Flow — Weight: 6

Check:

- The page has a clear progression section to section.
- Each section has a purpose.
- The experience builds momentum while scrolling.
- There is balance between intense and calm moments.
- The page avoids repetition or flat pacing.

## 5. Color System — Weight: 6

Check:

- The palette feels premium and intentional.
- Accent colors are controlled.
- Gradients, glows, and backgrounds feel cohesive.
- Contrast is strong enough for readability.
- Colors support mood and identity.

## 6. Typography — Weight: 8

Check:

- Font choices match the direction.
- Headlines feel strong and designed.
- Scale and spacing are well judged.
- Body text remains readable.
- Typography contributes to a premium feel.
- Text animation does not damage readability.

## 7. Layout & Composition — Weight: 8

Check:

- The layout has rhythm.
- Spacing feels intentional and premium.
- Hierarchy is clear.
- Composition feels balanced rather than cluttered.
- Layering and overlaps are controlled.
- White space is used effectively.

## 8. Motion Design / Animation Quality — Weight: 12

Check:

- Animations feel smooth and high quality.
- Motion supports the experience instead of distracting.
- Entrance timing feels deliberate.
- Scroll-based animation feels intentional.
- Hover and state transitions are polished.
- Motion hierarchy is clear.
- Effects are impressive without becoming excessive.
- The page is not over-animated.

## 9. Interaction Design — Weight: 8

Check:

- Interactive elements feel responsive.
- Hover states are meaningful and refined.
- Scroll cues or navigation cues are clear.
- The page is easy to explore despite heavy styling.
- Special interactions feel intuitive.
- Buttons, links, and cards provide satisfying feedback.

## 10. Visual Assets / Media Quality — Weight: 5

Check:

- Images, video, 3D, or particles are high quality.
- Assets match the art direction.
- Background media supports the page instead of cluttering it.
- The page avoids low-quality or mismatched visuals.
- Asset selection feels curated.

## 11. Performance — Weight: 10

Check:

- The page loads fast enough for a premium experience.
- Animations stay smooth on mid-range devices.
- Heavy assets are optimized.
- Scroll remains smooth.
- There are no obvious lag spikes or frame drops.
- JavaScript is not doing unnecessary work.
- Motion does not harm usability.
- Large media is lazy-loaded or optimized properly.

## 12. Responsive Design — Weight: 6

Check:

- Desktop feels intentional.
- Tablet remains strong.
- Mobile does not feel like a broken scaled-down version.
- Motion is simplified appropriately on smaller screens.
- Text stays readable across breakpoints.
- Layout integrity is preserved across devices.

## 13. Accessibility / Comfort — Weight: 5

Check:

- Text contrast is acceptable.
- Motion does not become uncomfortable.
- Reduced-motion behavior is supported.
- Important content remains understandable without effects.
- Focus states and keyboard basics are not ignored.

## 14. Technical Execution / Frontend Craft — Weight: 4

Check:

- The implementation feels production-grade.
- Motion logic is controlled cleanly.
- The stack is used appropriately for the effect level.
- The page feels stable rather than hacky.
- Polish suggests strong frontend craftsmanship.

## 15. Final Polish / Consistency — Weight: 3

Check:

- Nothing feels accidental or unfinished.
- Small details are polished.
- Quality is consistent across sections.
- There are no obvious weak sections.
- The page feels complete.

## 16. QA / Real-World Readiness — Weight: 5

Check:

- The page has been tested on multiple browsers.
- It has been checked on multiple screen sizes.
- Strong animations have been stress-tested.
- Loading behavior is acceptable.
- The experience works outside the creator's machine.

---

## Score Summary

- `90-100` — Top Tier
- `80-89` — Strong Premium Quality
- `70-79` — Good, But Not Exceptional
- `60-69` — Visually Decent, Execution Is Uneven
- `40-59` — Style-First, Not Quality-First
- `Below 40` — Not Ready
