# Scoring Anchors — Landing Page Showcase

Use these anchors to calibrate ratings before converting them into weighted scores.

Each section defines what `1/5`, `3/5`, and `5/5` look like. Use the nearest anchor and justify the score with observed evidence.

---

## 1. Creative Direction

- `1/5`: No clear concept. Styles conflict or feel generic.
- `3/5`: Clear direction exists, but not fully pushed through every section.
- `5/5`: Strong concept is immediately legible and sustained across the page.
- False positives: Trendy gradients or a dark palette alone do not equal direction.

## 2. Brand / Identity Expression

- `1/5`: Could belong to almost any brand.
- `3/5`: Some personality is visible, but the identity is not highly memorable.
- `5/5`: Distinct personality. The page feels authored and hard to confuse with another brand.
- False positives: A logo and color palette alone do not establish identity.

## 3. Hero Section Impact

- `1/5`: Weak first impression, no focal point, low urgency to continue.
- `3/5`: Clear hero with a reasonable focal point and headline, but limited emotional pull.
- `5/5`: Immediate impact, memorable focal point, and strong motivation to keep scrolling.
- False positives: Large type or a video background alone do not make a strong hero.

## 4. Storytelling / Section Flow

- `1/5`: Sections feel disconnected, repetitive, or flat.
- `3/5`: The page mostly flows, but pacing or transitions are uneven.
- `5/5`: The page builds momentum intentionally and avoids repetition fatigue.
- False positives: Many sections do not automatically create a story arc.

## 5. Color System

- `1/5`: Palette feels random, noisy, or weak in contrast.
- `3/5`: Palette is coherent and usable, but not especially refined or memorable.
- `5/5`: Color choices elevate mood, brand, hierarchy, and readability together.
- False positives: Bright accents and glow effects alone do not mean good color control.

## 6. Typography

- `1/5`: Font choice, sizing, or spacing harms readability or tone.
- `3/5`: Type is solid and readable, but lacks authority or precision.
- `5/5`: Font pairing, scale, rhythm, and readability all feel deliberate and premium.
- False positives: Fancy display fonts that hurt body readability should not score high.

## 7. Layout & Composition

- `1/5`: Cluttered or overly plain composition with weak hierarchy.
- `3/5`: Layout works and is clear, but rhythm or balance is only moderate.
- `5/5`: Strong hierarchy, premium spacing, and confident composition throughout.
- False positives: Off-grid layouts are not inherently premium unless they remain controlled.

## 8. Motion Design / Animation Quality

- `1/5`: Janky, distracting, repetitive, or overdone motion.
- `3/5`: Motion is smooth enough and supports the page, but lacks depth or hierarchy.
- `5/5`: Motion is polished, well timed, purposeful, and clearly prioritized by importance.
- False positives: More animation does not equal better motion quality.

## 9. Interaction Design

- `1/5`: Hover states, feedback, or navigation affordances feel weak or confusing.
- `3/5`: Interactions are functional and pleasant, but not especially refined.
- `5/5`: Interactions feel responsive, intuitive, and satisfyingly crafted.
- False positives: Clever cursor tricks should not inflate interaction scores if usability drops.

## 10. Visual Assets / Media Quality

- `1/5`: Media looks low resolution, mismatched, or decorative without purpose.
- `3/5`: Media quality is solid and aligned, but not standout.
- `5/5`: Assets are curated, high quality, and deeply integrated into the direction.
- False positives: More media or heavier media does not imply better asset quality.

## 11. Performance

- `1/5`: Noticeable lag, poor loading behavior, or obvious waste.
- `3/5`: Acceptable performance with some cost, but usable on realistic devices.
- `5/5`: Smooth loading, scrolling, and motion even under realistic mid-range conditions.
- False positives: Desktop smoothness on a powerful machine is not enough.

## 12. Responsive Design

- `1/5`: Mobile or tablet feel broken, cramped, or like afterthoughts.
- `3/5`: Layout adapts correctly with minor compromises.
- `5/5`: Each breakpoint feels intentional, legible, and visually preserved.
- False positives: Simply stacking desktop sections on mobile is not strong responsiveness.

## 13. Accessibility / Comfort

- `1/5`: Contrast, focus, or motion comfort is clearly neglected.
- `3/5`: Basics are mostly present, with some limitations.
- `5/5`: Accessibility and motion comfort are handled deliberately without reducing style.
- False positives: Passing visual contrast in one area does not cover the full experience.

## 14. Technical Execution / Frontend Craft

- `1/5`: Effects feel brittle, hacked together, or unstable.
- `3/5`: Implementation feels competent and production-capable.
- `5/5`: Frontend craft is notably strong, stable, and well chosen for the experience level.
- False positives: Expensive effects do not imply strong engineering.

## 15. Final Polish / Consistency

- `1/5`: Several details feel unfinished or inconsistent.
- `3/5`: Mostly polished, with a few weak spots.
- `5/5`: No accidental-feeling details. Craft is consistent from top to bottom.
- False positives: A polished hero does not cover weaker downstream sections.

## 16. QA / Real-World Readiness

- `1/5`: No sign of realistic testing; likely fragile outside the creator environment.
- `3/5`: Basic cross-browser and breakpoint confidence exists.
- `5/5`: Strong evidence of real-world validation, including stress-prone areas.
- False positives: "Works on my machine" is not QA readiness.

---

## Cross-Category Reminders

- A `5/5` score should be rare and defensible.
- If evidence is weak, do not force a high anchor. Downgrade status to `Provisional` or `Unverified` per `scoring-policy.md`.
- If a page is impressive but uneven, prefer clustered `3/5` and `4/5` scores over inflated `5/5` ratings.
